Elo Calculation Method

The total Elo score for a driver in a race is the sum of three components: Position Elo, Driver's Expected Elo, and Teammate Elo. Each component is designed to evaluate performance from a different perspective.

1. Position Elo

This score is based directly on the driver's finishing position. It rewards higher placements and penalizes lower ones. A DNF (Did Not Finish) results in a flat -5 penalty.

  • P1: +12
  • P2: +10
  • P3: +8
  • P4: +7
  • P5: +6
  • P6: +5
  • P7: +4
  • P8: +3
  • P9: +2
  • P10: +1
  • P11-P20: -1 to -10 respectively

2. Driver's Expected Elo

This component adjusts the score based on the pre-season ranking of the driver's team. It measures performance against expectation. Finishing higher than your team's expected range yields positive points, while finishing lower results in negative points. The table below shows the adjustments:

3. Teammate Elo

This provides a direct comparison to the driver's teammate in the same machinery. A driver receives a -4 penalty if they finish behind their teammate or if they DNF while their teammate finishes the race. This emphasizes the importance of beating the one driver with equal equipment.
